## LEGAL BASIS FOR DEMAND

### 1. North Carolina Landlord-Tenant Law (N.C.G.S. § 42-42)
North Carolina law requires landlords to:
- Maintain premises in habitable condition
- Make all repairs necessary to keep premises fit for human habitation
- Comply with applicable building and housing codes

**MAA has failed to meet these statutory obligations.**

---

### 2. Constructive Eviction Doctrine
Under North Carolina common law, a tenant may terminate a lease without penalty when:
1. Landlord breaches a duty to maintain premises
2. Breach substantially interferes with tenant's use and enjoyment
3. Tenant gives landlord notice and opportunity to repair
4. Landlord fails to remedy within reasonable time
5. Tenant vacates premises (or intends to vacate)

**All five elements are satisfied in my case.**
