## 🎯 **THE SCRIPT** (Memorize This)

> "Your Honor, this case is about a **22-month pattern**. From June 2024 to February 2026, I submitted over **40 work orders** for mold, HVAC failures, and water intrusion.
>
> **Exhibit A** shows the photos. **Exhibit B** shows the work orders. **Exhibit C** shows I paid **$37,400 in rent** despite these conditions.
>
> MAA didn't fix the problems. They **cancelled work orders**. That's not negligence—that's **organizational indifference**.
>
> North Carolina law says landlords must provide **habitable premises**. MAA didn't. I'm asking this Court to order **rent abatement** and award damages under **N.C.G.S. § 42-42** and **§ 1D-15**.
>
> Thank you."

## 🚨 **COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID**

### ❌ Don't:
- **Over-explain:** Save details for testimony/evidence
- **Apologize:** "I'm not a lawyer, but..." = weak start
- **Read from paper:** Eye contact with judge = credibility
- **Rush:** 2 minutes is enough if you're clear and direct
- **Get emotional:** Stick to facts, not feelings

### ✅ Do:
- **Lead with impact:** "22-month pattern" hooks attention
- **Quantify everything:** Numbers = concrete evidence
- **Cite statutes:** Shows you know the law
- **Request specific relief:** "Rent abatement + damages" (not vague "justice")
- **Stay calm:** Confidence = preparation

## 💡 **Pro Tips**

1. **Start strong:** First 15 seconds set tone. "This case is about a 22-month pattern" = powerful opening.

2. **Pause after exhibits:** Let judge register evidence. "Exhibit A shows photos [pause]. Exhibit B shows work orders [pause]."

3. **Emphasize "organizational indifference":** This phrase frames MAA's conduct as **systemic**, not isolated negligence.

4. **End with relief:** Judges appreciate clarity. "I'm asking for rent abatement + damages under §42-42 and §1D-15" = specific, actionable.

5. **If interrupted:** Stay calm. "Yes, Your Honor" → answer question → "May I continue?" → pick up where you left off.
