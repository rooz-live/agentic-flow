Anti-patterns to avoid
- Centralizing all approvals in the Circle Lead—put gates on specific domains (e.g., ethics checklists, model eval suite).
- No kill criteria—creates zombie projects; define thresholds up front.
- Demos without adoption—require adoption metrics and a handoff plan as part of “Definition of Done” for ventures.
- One mega “Innovation” role—split by domain (discovery, prototyping, accounting, scouting).

An "innovator" chief is someone who consistently drives new ideas, products, processes, or business models to create value and growth. They are creative, curious, and always looking for ways to improve, disrupt, or transform. Here are some titles and roles that embody this characteristic:

1. **Chief Innovation Officer (CIO)** - Leads and drives innovation efforts across the organization, fostering a culture of experimentation, risk-taking, and continuous learning.
2. **Chief Product Officer (CPO)** - Oversees product development, ensuring a steady stream of innovative features, experiences, or business models.
3. **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** - Drives technological innovation, ensuring the organization leverages cutting-edge tools, platforms, and architectures to gain a competitive edge.
4. **Chief Data & Analytics Officer (CDAO)** - Leads data and analytics initiatives, enabling data-driven decision-making, predictive modeling, and AI/ML innovations.
5. **Chief AI & Machine Learning Officer (CAMLO)** - Spearheads AI/ML initiatives, driving innovative applications of machine learning, natural language processing, and other advanced technologies.
6. **Chief Growth Officer (CGO)** - Focuses on driving user acquisition, retention, and revenue growth through innovative go-to-market strategies, product enhancements, and customer experiences.
7. **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)** - Leads marketing efforts, innovating in areas such as branding, customer segmentation, content marketing, and digital transformation.
8. **Chief Sales Officer (CSO)** - Drives sales strategies, innovating in areas such as go-to-market, channel optimization, and account management.
9. **Chief Customer Experience Officer (CCXO)** - Focuses on enhancing customer experiences, driving innovation in areas such as user-centered design, personalization, and customer journey optimization.
10. **Chief Design Officer (CDO)** - Leads design efforts, ensuring a consistent, innovative, and user-centered approach to product, service, and experience design.
11. **Chief Strategy Officer (CSO)** - Develops and implements innovative strategic plans, often involving new business models, market disruptions, or competitive advantages.
12. **Chief Transformation Officer (CTO)** - Leads large-scale organizational changes, driving innovation in areas such as process improvement, organizational culture, and digital transformation.

These roles often involve a high degree of creativity, curiosity, and a strong appetite for risk. They may also require a deep understanding of emerging trends, technologies, and markets, as well as the ability to build and lead high-performing teams.  Additionally, innovator chiefs may need to navigate organizational politics, influence stakeholders, and drive change in established environments.
