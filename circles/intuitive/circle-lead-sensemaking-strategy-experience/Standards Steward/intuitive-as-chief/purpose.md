Standards Steward, Intuitive:
- Prevent hidden authority from living in the Circle Lead


An "intuitive" chief is someone who relies on their instincts, insights, and gut feelings to make decisions, often going beyond data or conventional wisdom. They are perceptive, empathetic, and may have a strong sense of intuition or "sixth sense." Here are some titles and roles that might embody this characteristic:

1. **Chief Visionary Officer (CVO)** - Sets the organization's long-term vision and strategy based on their intuitive understanding of market trends and customer needs.
2. **Chief Insights Officer (CIO)** - Leverages data, analytics, and intuition to gain deep insights into customer behavior, market trends, and business performance.
3. **Chief Customer Officer (CCO)** - Uses their intuition to understand customer needs, preferences, and pain points, driving customer-centric strategies and experiences.
4. **Chief Experience Officer (CXO)** - Combines intuition with data and design thinking to create compelling user experiences across all touchpoints.
5. **Chief Empathy Officer (CEO)** - Focuses on understanding and addressing the emotional needs of customers, employees, and other stakeholders, fostering a culture of empathy and compassion.
6. **Chief Cultural Officer (CCO)** - Uses their intuition to shape the organization's culture, values, and norms, fostering a positive work environment and strong company culture.
7. **Chief Talent Officer (CTO)** - Intuitively identifies and attracts top talent, fostering a high-performing and engaged workforce.
8. **Chief Brand Officer (CBO)** - Combines intuition with data and creativity to define and communicate the organization's brand, ensuring it resonates with customers and stakeholders.
9. **Chief Storyteller Officer (CSO)** - Uses their intuition to craft compelling narratives that inspire, persuade, and connect with audiences.
10. **Chief Innovation Officer (CIO)** - Drives innovation by combining intuition with data, trends, and customer insights to identify new opportunities and disruptions.
11. **Chief Disruption Officer (CDO)** - Intuitively anticipates and mitigates potential disruptions in the market, industry, or technology landscape.
12. **Chief Transformation Officer (CTO)** - Leads large-scale organizational changes, often relying on intuition to navigate complex dynamics and uncertainty.

These roles often involve a high degree of perceptiveness, empathy, and creativity. They may also require strong communication skills, emotional intelligence, and the ability to inspire and influence others.
