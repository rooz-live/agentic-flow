Standards Steward, Exploration/Discovery Seeker:
- Prevent hidden authority from living in the Circle Lead
- Avoid “chief” to keep decision rights tied to domains and artifacts, not a person.
- Seeking is artifact-centric (signals logs, opportunity backlogs, pilot charters); P/D/A keeps it visible and actionable.
- Decision rights stay with roles that own the relevant domains (e.g., partner pilot charters), avoiding hidden gatekeeping.
- Rep/Lead Links resolve cross-circle tensions so seeking feeds delivery without hierarchy.

Anti-patterns to avoid
- Guru “seeker” with broad veto. Keep domains explicit; pair with Experiment Framer and Portfolio Steward for evidence-based decisions.
- Endless scouting with no conversion. Require probe design and tripwires for each high-priority opportunity.
- Single mega “Seeker” role. Split by domains (signals, partners, markets, tech) to prevent bottlenecks.

Role: Signals & Horizon Scanner
- Purpose: We notice and act on shifts before they’re obvious.
- Domains: “/strategy/signals”, quarterly scenario briefs, tripwire indicators.
- Accountabilities:
  - Curate external signals; tag by plausibility/impact/timeframe.
  - Produce quarterly scenarios with options and early indicators.
  - Propose tripwires and option plays to relevant circles.

Role: Partner & Alliance Scout
- Purpose: External leverage accelerates learning and impact.
- Domains: Partner pipeline, NDAs/MoUs, pilot charters, evaluation rubric.
- Accountabilities:
  - Source and qualify partners against strategy and standards.
  - Design time-boxed pilots with clear success/kill criteria.
  - Publish keep/kill recommendations with evidence and risks.

A "seeker" chief is someone who actively pursues new opportunities, innovations, or growth. They are curious, proactive, and always looking for ways to improve, expand, or disrupt. Here are some titles and roles that embody this characteristic:

1. **Chief Innovation Officer (CIO)** - Drives research, development, and adoption of new technologies, processes, or business models.
2. **Chief Growth Officer (CGO)** - Focuses on driving user acquisition, retention, and revenue growth through experimentation, optimization, and innovation.
3. **Chief Disruption Officer (CDO)** - Proactively identifies and mitigates potential disruptions in the market, industry, or technology landscape.
4. **Chief Transformation Officer (CTO)** - Leads large-scale organizational changes, often involving new technologies, processes, or business models.
5. **Chief Strategy Officer (CSO)** - Develops and implements strategic plans to achieve organizational goals, often involving innovative approaches.
6. **Chief Data & Analytics Officer (CDAO)** - Drives data-driven decision-making, often involving new tools, techniques, or data sources.
7. **Chief AI & Machine Learning Officer (CAMLO)** - Leads the development and implementation of AI and machine learning solutions to drive business value.
8. **Chief Product Officer (CPO)** - Oversees product development, often involving new features, user experiences, or business models.
9. **Chief Marketing Officer (CMO)** - Drives marketing strategies, often involving new channels, tactics, or technologies.
10. **Chief Sales Officer (CSO)** - Leads sales efforts, often involving new markets, products, or go-to-market strategies.
11. **Chief Customer Experience Officer (CCXO)** - Focuses on enhancing customer experiences, often involving new touchpoints, channels, or technologies.
12. **Chief Sustainability Officer (CSO)** - Drives sustainability initiatives, often involving new practices, technologies, or partnerships.

These roles often involve a high degree of curiosity, proactivity, and a strong appetite for risk. They may also require a deep understanding of emerging trends, technologies, and markets.
