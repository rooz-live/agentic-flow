## Consolidation Analysis

### ✅ MOTION TO CONSOLIDATE: Case #1 + Case #3

**Reasoning:**
1. **Common Question of Law/Fact:** Employment history directly impacts lease approval and income verification
2. **Interdependent Evidence:** Employment blocking (Case #3) caused housing instability (Case #1)
   - Plaintiff unable to provide stable employment verification for lease
   - Landlord (MAA) likely required employment/income docs
   - Employment dispute (2019-2024) coincides with housing crisis
3. **Judicial Economy:** Single trial avoids duplicative testimony on income, employment, financial capacity
4. **Rule:** NC Rules of Civil Procedure Rule 42(a) - "Consolidation when common question of law or fact exists"

**Timing:**
- **Option A:** File Motion to Consolidate AFTER Trial #1 (if plaintiff prevails or trial continues)
- **Option B:** File Motion to Consolidate FOR Trial #2 (if Trial #1 generates additional proceedings)
- **Option C:** Raise related claims during Trial #1 (if judge permits amendment of complaint)

**Risks:**
- Consolidation may delay Trial #1 (judge may order continuance to allow Apex to respond)
- Employment case may require different venue (VA vs NC jurisdiction)

## Supporting Case Law (NC)

- **NC R. Civ. P. 42(a):** "When actions involve a common question of law or fact, the court may order consolidation"
