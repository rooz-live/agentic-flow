# Opening Statement Quick Reference Card

**Print this page and bring to courtroom** (Single 8.5x11 sheet)

---

## 🎯 OPENING (15 sec)
"Your Honor, this case is about a landlord's material breach of the implied warranty of habitability under North Carolina General Statute 42-42."

---

## 📋 FACTS (60 sec)

### Mold (Hold up H-1)
"For six months—September 2025 through February 2026—I lived with severe habitability defects that the landlord refused to repair despite repeated written notice."

"**Exhibit H-1** shows black mold covering **forty percent** of my bathroom wall. This wasn't surface mildew—this was systemic contamination requiring professional remediation."

### Heating (Point to H-2)
"**Exhibit H-2** documents heating system failure. No heat for **six consecutive weeks** in January 2026. Indoor temperatures at 48 degrees Fahrenheit."

### Maintenance (Point to H-3)
"**Exhibit H-3** contains **forty-plus maintenance requests** I submitted from June 2025 through January 2026. The landlord's response? Delays, deferrals, and eventually—silence."

### Notice (Show H-4)
"**Exhibit H-4** shows certified mail receipts proving I sent written notice to MAA management on December 15, 2025. **Sixty days passed** with no meaningful response."

---

## ⚖️ LEGAL STANDARD (30 sec)
"N.C.G.S. § 42-42 establishes an implied warranty of habitability in all residential leases. When a landlord fails to maintain premises in habitable condition after reasonable notice, the tenant is entitled to rent abatement and damages."

"The defendant chose non-response over repair. That constitutes **material breach**."

---

## 🎯 REQUEST (25 sec)
"I respectfully request the Court find MAA in breach of the implied warranty of habitability and award:

1. Rent abatement for six months of uninhabitable conditions
2. Damages for health impacts and loss of use
3. Attorney fees and court costs as permitted by statute

Thank you, Your Honor."

---

## 🎭 IF JUDGE ASKS

### "Why didn't you move out?"
"Your Honor, I was under economic pressure at the time. Once I discovered the defects, I followed the statutory process: written notice, reasonable cure opportunity, then legal action."

### "Can you afford the rent?"
"Your Honor, the issue before the Court today is whether the landlord breached the warranty of habitability. The photographic evidence shows they did not maintain habitable premises."

### "What damages?"
"Rent abatement for six months of uninhabitable conditions—September 2025 through February 2026. Based on monthly rent of $3,400, that would be partial abatement calculated pro-rata, approximately $12,240."

### "Did you pay rent?"
"Yes, Your Honor. I continued paying rent during the entire period while attempting to resolve these issues. That's part of why I'm seeking rent abatement now—I paid for a habitable unit but received an uninhabitable one."
