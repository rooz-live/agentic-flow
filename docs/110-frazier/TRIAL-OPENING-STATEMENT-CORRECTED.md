## 📋 OPENING STATEMENT (FULL TEXT)

Your Honor,

This case is about a landlord's material breach of the implied warranty of habitability under North Carolina General Statute 42-42.

**The Facts:**

For six months—from September 2025 through February 2026—I lived in an apartment at 110 Frazier Avenue with severe habitability defects that the landlord, Mid-Atlantic Apartments, refused to repair despite repeated written notice.

**Exhibit H-1** shows black mold covering forty percent of my bathroom wall. This wasn't surface mildew—this was systemic contamination requiring professional remediation.

**Exhibit H-2** documents heating system failure. No heat for six consecutive weeks in January 2026. Indoor temperatures at 48 degrees Fahrenheit. I have utility bills showing my attempts to compensate with space heaters.

**Exhibit H-3** contains forty-plus maintenance requests I submitted through the MAA tenant portal from June 2025 through January 2026. Requests for mold remediation, plumbing repairs, heating repair. The landlord's response? Delays, deferrals, and eventually—silence.

Under North Carolina law, I was required to provide written notice and reasonable opportunity to cure before pursuing legal action. **Exhibit H-4** shows certified mail receipts proving I sent written notice to MAA management on December 15, 2025. Sixty days passed with no meaningful response.

**The Legal Standard:**

N.C.G.S. § 42-42 establishes an implied warranty of habitability in all residential leases. When a landlord fails to maintain premises in habitable condition after reasonable notice, the tenant is entitled to rent abatement and damages.

The defendant chose non-response over repair. That constitutes material breach.

**What I'm Asking:**

I respectfully request the Court find MAA in breach of the implied warranty of habitability and award:
1. Rent abatement for the six months of uninhabitable conditions
2. Damages for health impacts and loss of use
3. Attorney fees and court costs as permitted by statute

Thank you, Your Honor.
