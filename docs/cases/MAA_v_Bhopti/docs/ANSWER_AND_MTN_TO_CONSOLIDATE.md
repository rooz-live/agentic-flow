## DEFENDANT’S ANSWER AND AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES TO COMPLAINT IN SUMMARY EJECTMENT, COUNTERCLAIMS, AND MOTION TO CONSOLIDATE

NOW COMES Defendant, Shahrooz Bhopti (hereinafter “Defendant” or “Tenant”), representing himself *pro se*, and responding to the Complaint in Summary Ejectment filed by Plaintiff, Mid-America Apartments, L.P. d/b/a MAA (hereinafter “Plaintiff” or “Landlord”).

Defendant asserts that this is an unlawful retaliatory eviction based on Defendant's exercise of protected rights, including complaints regarding severe habitability defects and systemic indifference on the part of Plaintiff's property management.

### MOTION TO CONSOLIDATE
Defendant moves this Court to consolidate this summary ejectment action with any and all related claims concerning the lease agreement and habitability of the premises, including but not limited to small claims actions for rent abatement or damages arising from Plaintiff's failure to maintain fit and habitable premises pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-42. Consolidating these actions serves judicial economy and prevents inconsistent verdicts.

### FIRST DEFENSE (Answer to Complaint)
Defendant admits, denies, or lacks sufficient information to respond to the allegations in Plaintiff's Complaint as follows:
1. Defendant admits entering into a residential lease agreement for the premises located at 417 Tryon Rd, Raleigh, NC 27603.
2. Defendant denies that he is in breach of the lease agreement that would justify summary ejectment. Any alleged withholding of rent was legally justified under North Carolina law due to Plaintiff's material breach of the implied warranty of habitability.
3. Defendant denies the remaining allegations of the Complaint that assert Defendant is unlawfully holding over or owes the amount claimed without offset.

### SECOND DEFENSE (Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability)
Pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-42, Plaintiff had a statutory duty to provide a fit and habitable premises. During the tenancy, Plaintiff failed to correct severe and ongoing habitability defects, including but not limited to:
* Unresolved plumbing failures resulting in continuous moisture and mold risks.
* Failure to provide operable appliances originally included in the lease.
* Persistent pest infestations ignored by management.
Because of Plaintiff's material breach of the lease and statutory duties, Defendant is entitled to a complete or partial abatement of rent.

### THIRD DEFENSE (Retaliatory Eviction)
Pursuant to N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-37.1, Plaintiff’s summary ejectment action is retaliatory. Defendant made multiple good-faith complaints to Plaintiff's agents regarding the habitability of the premises and requested repairs. After Defendant asserted his rights, Plaintiff aggressively pursued eviction rather than remedying the issues in a timely and professional manner.

### FOURTH DEFENSE (Systemic Indifference and Unfair Trade Practices)
Plaintiff's conduct constitutes a pattern of *Systemic Indifference* and unfair and deceptive trade practices under N.C. Gen. Stat. § 75-1.1. Plaintiff routinely uses summary ejectment as an operational tool to clear unresolved maintenance ledgers and retaliate against tenants who demand safe housing, rather than fulfilling their contractual and statutory obligations.

### COUNTERCLAIMS
Defendant asserts the following counterclaims against Plaintiff:
1. **Breach of Contract (Implied Warranty of Habitability):** For Plaintiff's failure to maintain the premises in a fit and habitable condition, resulting in diminished rental value.
2. **Retaliatory Eviction:** For actual and statutory damages resulting from Plaintiff's retaliatory conduct under N.C. Gen. Stat. § 42-37.1.
3. **Unfair and Deceptive Trade Practices:** Due to Plaintiff's systemic and coercive property management practices designed to extract rent without providing basic habitable conditions.

---
### PRAYER FOR RELIEF
WHEREFORE, Defendant prays the Court as follows:
1. That this action be consolidated with any ongoing or future claims related to the tenancy and habitability of the premises.
2. That Plaintiff’s Complaint in Summary Ejectment be dismissed with prejudice.
3. That judgment be entered in favor of Defendant on all counterclaims.
4. That Defendant be awarded damages for rent abatement.
5. That Defendant be awarded treble damages for Plaintiff's unfair and deceptive trade practices.
6. That Defendant be awarded costs and any other relief the Court deems just and proper.
