## Quaternary Perspectives (Unknown-Unknown Unknowns)

### 16. Black Swan Events
- **Examples**: 
  - MAA bankruptcy/acquisition (changes settlement dynamics)
  - Sudden regulatory change (new habitability standards)
  - Parallel criminal investigation (fraud, negligence)
  - Building closure order (validates uninhabitability claim)
- **Anti-fragile**: Position to benefit from chaos

### 17. Cognitive Biases (all parties)
- **Anchoring**: First number mentioned becomes reference
- **Confirmation bias**: Seek evidence supporting pre-existing beliefs
- **Recency bias**: Latest evidence weighted more heavily
- **Availability heuristic**: Vivid examples (mold photos) > statistics
- **Sunk cost fallacy**: Defendant's prior investments in defense

### 18. Procedural Surprises
- **Examples**:
  - Judge recusal (new judge, reset strategy)
  - Sudden settlement conference (pressure to resolve)
  - Summary judgment motion (case ends early)
  - Continuance granted (more prep time or momentum loss)

### 19. Evidence Discovery Shocks
- **Plaintiff side**: Smoking gun email/memo (neglect policy)
- **Defense side**: Plaintiff's prior complaints dismissed elsewhere
- **Impact**: Case value shifts dramatically

### 20. Technology/AI Considerations
- **Neural Trader**: Could be seen as novelty/gimmick OR impressive innovation
- **AI Bias**: Judge/jury unfamiliarity could help or hurt
- **Expert Challenge**: "This is just a demo, not real AI"
