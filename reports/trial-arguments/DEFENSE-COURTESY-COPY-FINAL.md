<div class="section">
<h2>⚖️ LEGAL AUTHORITIES</h2>

<div class="box">
<h3>1. Von Pettis Realty v. McKoy</h3>
<p><strong>Holding:</strong> When residential unit is uninhabitable, tenant owes zero rent.</p>
<p><strong>Plaintiff's Use:</strong> "Your Honor, Von Pettis is clear — 22 months of mold, HVAC failures, and electrical hazards = uninhabitable unit = zero rent owed."</p>
<p><strong>Defense Response:</strong> Distinguish by arguing issues were minor or tenant caused delays. Challenge "uninhabitable" standard.</p>
</div>

<div class="box">
<h3>2. N.C.G.S. § 42-42 (Implied Warranty of Habitability)</h3>
<p><strong>Standard:</strong> Landlord must maintain fit premises; tenant may withhold rent or sue for breach.</p>
<p><strong>Plaintiff's Use:</strong> "40+ work orders over 22 months = systemic breach."</p>
<p><strong>Defense Response:</strong> Argue repairs attempted in good faith, issues resolved within reasonable time.</p>
</div>

<div class="box">
<h3>3. N.C.G.S. § 42-37.1 (Retaliatory Eviction)</h3>
<p><strong>Standard:</strong> Eviction within 12 months of habitability complaint = rebuttable presumption of retaliation.</p>
<p><strong>Plaintiff's Use:</strong> "Eviction filed Feb 9, 2026 during settlement talks = textbook retaliation."</p>
<p><strong>Defense Response:</strong> <span class="highlight">CRITICAL.</span> Must rebut presumption by proving eviction based solely on non-payment, not retaliation. Email timing makes this difficult.</p>
</div>

<div class="box">
<h3>4. N.C.G.S. § 1D-15 (Punitive Damages)</h3>
<p><strong>Standard:</strong> 3x compensatory for willful/wanton conduct; cap of greater of 3x compensatory or $250,000.</p>
<p><strong>Plaintiff's Use:</strong> "Systemically canceling 40+ work orders over 22 months = willful neglect."</p>
<p><strong>Defense Response:</strong> Argue conduct was negligent at most, not willful. Challenge organizational failure theory.</p>
</div>
</div>
