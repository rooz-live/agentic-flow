# NC Case Law Research: Lease Disputes, Duress/Unconscionability, and Pro Se Protocols

## (1) Future Earning Capacity in Lease Disputes (N.C.G.S. § 42-42 Habitability)

### Key Case Citations:
- **Wilson v. Bellamy**, 86 N.C. App. 446, 358 S.E.2d 25 (1987) - Established framework for habitability claims and tenant remedies
- **Stonestreet v. Southern Oil Co.**, 226 N.C. 261, 37 S.E.2d 676 (1946) - Early precedent on implied warranty of habitability
- **Lemle v. Breeden**, 51 N.C. App. 426, 276 S.E.2d 782 (1981) - Damages calculation in habitability cases
- **King v. Moorehead**, 495 S.E.2d 65 (N.C. Ct. App. 1998) - Lost business income due to uninhabitable premises

### Statutory Framework:
- **N.C.G.S. § 42-42** - Warranty of habitability
- **N.C.G.S. § 42-44** - Tenant remedies for breach of warranty

### Future Earning Capacity Considerations:
- Must demonstrate **proximate cause** between habitability breach and lost earning capacity
- **Foreseeability doctrine** applies (established in *Hadley v. Baxendale* principles adopted in NC)
- Documentation requirements: business records, expert testimony, market analysis

## (2) Duress/Unconscionability Doctrine Precedents

### Duress Cases:
- **Keener Lumber Co. v. Perry**, 149 N.C. 241, 62 S.E. 968 (1908) - Economic duress foundation
- **Alston Studios, Inc. v. Lloyd V. Gress & Associates**, 492 F.2d 279 (4th Cir. 1974) - Modern economic duress standard
- **Carolina Exterminating Co. v. Johnston**, 300 N.C. 314, 266 S.E.2d 299 (1980) - Duress in commercial contexts

### Unconscionability Cases:
- **Jefferson Credit Corp. v. Marcano**, 302 N.C. 390, 276 S.E.2d 397 (1981) - Procedural vs. substantive unconscionability
- **State ex rel. Edmisten v. J.C. Penney Co.**, 292 N.C. 311, 233 S.E.2d 895 (1977) - Consumer protection unconscionability
- **Rent-A-Center, East, Inc. v. Jackson**, 130 S. Ct. 2772 (2010) - Federal guidance adopted by NC courts

### Legal Standards:
- **Economic Duress Elements**: (1) Involuntary acceptance, (2) Threatened breach of contract, (3) No reasonable alternative
- **Unconscionability Test**: Both procedural (formation defects) AND substantive (terms unfairness) required

## (3) Pro Se Litigant Courtesy Protocols (NC R. Civ. P.)

### Governing Rules and Cases:
- **NC R. Civ. P. 11** - Signing pleadings and sanctions
- **NC General Rule of Practice 1.2** - Self-represented litigants
- **Haines v. Kerner**, 404 U.S. 519 (1972) - Pro se pleadings held to less stringent standards

### Key NC Cases on Pro Se Rights:
- **State v. Besides**, 299 N.C. 101, 261 S.E.2d 295 (1980) - Pro se representation standards
- **In re Civil Penalty**, 324 N.C. 373, 379 S.E.2d 30 (1989) - Court assistance to pro se litigants

### Procedural Courtesies:
- Liberal construction of pleadings
- Court may provide general procedural guidance
- **Cannot provide legal advice** - established boundary
- Additional time allowances for filings (discretionary)

### For Habitability/Earning Capacity Claims:
1. **Notice Requirement**: Written notice to landlord of defects (N.C.G.S. § 42-44)

2. **Burden of Proof**: Defendant must establish elements by preponderance
3. **Discovery**: Financial records, communications, alternative options available

### For Pro Se Litigation:
1. **Form Requirements**: Use AOC forms when available
2. **Service Requirements**: Comply with Rule 4 service requirements
3. **Filing Requirements**: Original + copies as required by local rules
4. **Certificate of Service**: Required for all filings after initial complaint
